  (** Quoting nLab:

      If [C] and [D] are categories, a profunctor from [C] to [D] is a functor [D·µí·µñ * C -> Set]. Such a profunctor is usually written as [F : C ‚á∏ D].

      Every functor [f : C -> D] induces two profunctors [D(1, f) : C ‚á∏ D] and [D(f, 1) : D ‚á∏ C], defined by [D(1,f)(d,c) = D(d, f(c))] and [D(f, 1)(c,d) = D(f(c), d)]. These profunctors are called representable (or sometimes one of them is corepresentable).

      In particular the identity profunctor [Id : C ‚á∏ C] is represented by the identity functor and hence is given by the hom-functor [C(‚àí, ‚àí) : C·µí·µñ * C -> Set].

      The notion generalizes to many other kinds of categories. For instance, if [C] and [D] are enriched over some symmetric closed monoidal category [V], then a profunctor from [C] to [D] is a [V]-functor [D·µí·µñ ‚äó C -> V]. If they are internal categories, then a profunctor [C ‚á∏ D] is an internal diagram on [D·µí·µñ * C], and so on. There are also other equivalent definitions in each case; see below.

      A profunctor is also sometimes called a (bi)module or a distributor or a correspondence, though the latter word is also used for a span. The term ‚Äúmodule‚Äù tends to be common in Australia, especially in the enriched case; here the intuition is that for one-object [V]-categories, i.e. monoids in [V], profunctors really are the same as bimodules between such monoids in the usual sense. ‚ÄúProfunctor‚Äù is perhaps more common in the Set-based and internal cases (but is also used in the enriched case); here the intuition is that a profunctor is a generalization of a functor, via the construction of ‚Äúrepresentable‚Äù profunctors. Jean B√©nabou, who invented the term and originally used ‚Äúprofunctor,‚Äù now prefers ‚Äúdistributor,‚Äù which is supposed to carry the intuition that a distributor generalizes a functor in a similar way to how a distribution generalizes a function.

      Note that the convention that a profunctor is a functor [D·µí·µñ * C -> Set] is not universal; some authors reverse C and D and/or put the ‚Äúop‚Äù on the other one. See the discussion below. *)

End profunctor.
